How It Works

Three simple steps. No wiring degree required.

1

Mount the Lock Box

Attach the weatherproof lock box near your battery. Connect the two power leads inline with your main battery cable. Zip ties and mounting bracket included.

2

Pair Your Keys

Hold both keycards near the lock box and press the pairing button. The box and keys exchange encrypted credentials. Takes 10 seconds. Done once, permanent bond.

3

Ride Secure

Walk up with your keycard in your pocket. The lock box detects it, closes the relay, and power flows. Walk away and it locks automatically after 30 seconds.

Security Architecture

How we prevent your bike from being hacked.

🔐

Unique Key Pairing

Each lock box generates a unique 256-bit identity at the factory. During pairing, the keycard and lock box perform an ECDH key exchange. The shared secret never leaves either device. Even we can't clone your key.

🔀

Rolling Codes

Every unlock sends a different encrypted code. The lock box and keycard stay in sync with a counter. Capturing a signal does nothing - it's already expired. Same tech as modern car key fobs.

🛡

Replay Protection

Challenge-response protocol: the lock box sends a random challenge, the keycard signs it with the shared secret. An attacker would need to break AES-256 to forge a response. That would take billions of years.

💥

Tamper Resistance

Electronics are potted in epoxy - cutting the box open destroys the circuits. The relay defaults to OFF (locked). Cutting the wires = bike stays dead. No bypass without the paired key.

FAQ

What e-motos does this work with?

Any electric moto, scooter, or e-bike with a battery voltage between 12V and 72V. The lock box sits inline with the main power cable. If your bike has a positive and negative wire from battery to controller, MotoKey works.

Can I add more keys later?

Yes. Enter pairing mode on the lock box and hold the new keycard nearby. The lock box supports up to 8 paired keys. You can also unpair lost keys by entering a factory-reset sequence (requires physical access to the pair button).

What if my keycard battery dies?

The lock box has a backup NFC mode. Hold the dead keycard directly against the lock box (within 2cm) and the box powers the card via NFC to complete authentication. You'll want to replace the battery soon, but you won't be stranded.

How hard is the install?

15-20 minutes with basic tools. Disconnect battery, cut the main positive wire, strip ends, connect to lock box leads (screw terminals), mount with bracket or zip ties, reconnect battery, pair your keys. Full video guide included.

What happens if someone cuts the wires?

The bike stays dead. The relay defaults to OPEN (no power). Cutting the lock box wires just permanently disconnects the battery from the motor. An attacker would need to bypass the lock box entirely by splicing new wire around it - which requires disassembling the bike and accessing the internal wiring.
